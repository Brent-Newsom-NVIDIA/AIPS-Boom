## Summary
Kroger is a major U.S. grocery and retail company used in the wiki as a named customer case study for Everseen checkout loss prevention on NVIDIA-accelerated edge infrastructure. The Lenovo case study says Kroger used Everseen Visual AI, Lenovo Edge AI servers, and NVIDIA GPUs to improve self-checkout accuracy and reduce retail shrink.

## Detail
The case study describes Kroger as headquartered in Cincinnati, Ohio and operating supermarkets, pharmacies, fuel centers, and multi-department stores across the United States. It frames Kroger's self-checkout challenge around barcode scanning errors, missed scans, customer friction, and stock-loss risk.

In [[Kroger-Everseen-Checkout-Loss-Prevention]], Kroger uses Everseen Visual AI to analyze checkout video and POS data in real time. The platform prompts shoppers to self-correct scanning errors and alerts associates when assistance is needed, giving Kroger more control over self-checkout operations while preserving customer experience.

Instacart also names Kroger among retailers offering Caper Carts powered by [[NVIDIA-Jetson-Platform]]. [[CoolerX-Kroger-NVIDIA-In-Store-Retail-Media]] adds Kroger context for in-store retail media and smart screens, using the NVIDIA GTC Cooler Screens session and CoolerX public deployment material. Keep the Everseen/Lenovo/NVIDIA T4 case as Kroger's strongest detailed loss-prevention case study; use the Caper and CoolerX links as supporting evidence for smart carts, in-store engagement, and retail media workloads.
