## Summary
Centific published an anonymized customer story for a leading home improvement retailer using computer vision and NVIDIA edge computing to reduce checkout-related inventory shrinkage. The customer is not named, so this should be treated as strong anonymized deployment proof rather than a named-customer case study.

## Detail
The Centific story frames the workload around inventory shrinkage at the point of sale. Centific integrated one IP camera per checkout lane and used NVIDIA edge computing for real-time communications, allowing the customer to detect potential shrinkage during transactions.

For recommendations, use this page when a retailer asks about loss prevention, checkout-lane monitoring, real-time inventory monitoring, or a broader store-operations platform that combines computer vision with workflow automation. The source reports 175 participating stores, 180M in annual shrinkage reduction, and 75 labor hours saved per week, making this a useful proof point even though the retailer is anonymized.

This is most relevant to [[NVIDIA-Metropolis]] and [[NVIDIA-DeepStream]] style store-camera analytics, with [[NVIDIA-Jetson-Platform]] or edge GPU infrastructure as the site deployment pattern. It also supports the [[Centific]] retail ISV recommendation where the customer needs more than a single detection model and wants an operational platform for shrink, inventory, safety, and workforce workflows.
